The Lighthouse Keeper's Dog

Every evening, Bruno climbed the ninety-nine steps of the lighthouse with old Mr. Larsen. While the keeper polished the great lamp, Bruno would sit by the window and watch the fishing boats come home.

One stormy night, the lamp flickered and went out. Mr. Larsen searched his pockets for matches, but they were empty. Bruno barked twice, ran down the stairs, and returned with the tin box the keeper kept by the door — the one place he always forgot to look.

That night, three boats found their way safely into the harbour, and Bruno got an extra sausage for dinner.
